## What changed on June 21, 2026

Work shifted from visible feature progress to foundation verification after the local development environment showed enough uncertainty that further building would have increased risk rather than produced trustworthy progress.

The weekend produced a safer and more reviewable foundation:

- Python, Node, npm, WSL access, Ollama, and project pins were verified.
- The primary Hermes installation was baselined read-only.
- Hermes source inspection identified the supported isolation controls.
- Independent architecture defense rejected unsafe assumptions in the first isolation plan.
- A safer sequential Phase 1A design was approved for controlled validation.

## Why it matters

The NightSchool Study Assistant became the first controlled proving ground for a more disciplined operating model: scoped requirements, architecture review before mutation, backup planning, timestamped handoffs, and evidence attached to completion claims.

That means the value of the work was not a claim that the product had already shipped. The value was converting an ambiguous environment into a verified baseline and a safer executable plan.
